## The discipline layer

**gstack** is the pack of skills and workflows every lane runs on. If a lane is a working agent, gstack
is the set of professional habits it can't skip. It turns "an AI wrote some code" into a repeatable
process with checkpoints — the same one a careful engineering team would use.

## Plan → review → ship

gstack organises work into named steps a lane invokes as it goes:

- **Plan** — before anything large or ambiguous, the work is shaped and pressure-tested (a CEO- or
  engineering-level review of the plan) so effort goes at the right thing.
- **Review** — before a PR, the change gets a staff-engineer-style audit and a QA pass: does it do
  what the ticket asked, is it correct, does it hold up.
- **Ship** — the change is finalised into a clean PR, and the lane stops for a human to merge.

There's a step for closing out a phase too (a retro), so the system keeps learning. The point of all
of it is that quality isn't left to chance on any single change — the same gates apply every time.

## Shared across the portfolio

gstack isn't bespoke to one venture. It's the common substrate every Bruntsfield lane uses, which is
why work looks consistent across ventures and why improvements to the method benefit all of them at
once. Your venture gets a battle-tested process from day one instead of inventing its own.
