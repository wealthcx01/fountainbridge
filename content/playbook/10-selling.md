# Selling — interest, not interruption

Most startup selling advice is about volume: more outreach, more cold email, more "top of funnel."
Foundry ventures reject that — not only because it's low-yield, but because we ratified a stance
(`docs/research-gtm.md`): **agents email only people who have signalled interest.** Cold outreach is
de-scoped entirely. That is a legal position (consent / soft opt-in under PECR and GDPR) and a
strategic one: selling to demand you created beats manufacturing attention you didn't.

## The principle

You are not trying to convince strangers. You are trying to be *ready and specific* for the people who
already raised a hand — an ad response, a waitlist signup, an enquiry, an event registration. Everything
below serves that.

## The path

1. **Create a reason to raise a hand.** Content, a useful free thing, a sharp point of view — the
   "soft introduction" this playbook itself models. Interest is the top of a Foundry funnel because
   consent is the only lawful and durable entry.
2. **Classify every contact by interest source.** Who they are, what they signalled, the lawful basis.
   This isn't bureaucracy — it's the difference between a personal reply and spam, and it's recorded as
   an approval event.
3. **Reply like a human, because it is one.** 1:1 replies to flagged interest go from the founder's
   venture-domain address, agent-drafted, human-gated. They should read like the founder wrote them at
   their desk — because the founder approved them, and the identity is genuinely theirs.
4. **Separate the streams.** Personal 1:1 replies use the primary domain. Bulk, lifecycle, and
   newsletter sends go from a dedicated subdomain (`mail.<venture>.com`) so the apex domain's reputation
   is never exposed to volume. SPF/DKIM/DMARC from day one.
5. **Gate every external send.** Nothing leaves without a recorded human approval: recipient
   classification, lawful basis, suppression check, the exact draft, the approver, the timestamp, the
   sending identity. In the studio this is the attention queue and (from Phase 4) the ActiveGraph gate.

## Why this is also the better *strategy*

Consent-first selling isn't a compliance tax — it's a [moat](moats). It forces you to earn genuine
demand (branding, cornered relationships), it keeps your sending reputation clean (a real asset), and
it makes every venture's GTM auditable by construction. The audit log *is* the accountability record
regulators want, produced as a side effect of doing it right.

> The best outreach is the reply you were ready to send the moment someone asked.
