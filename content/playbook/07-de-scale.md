> **Disciplined Entrepreneurship** (Bill Aulet). Our own application of Aulet's method. Step 24 and the
> move beyond the beachhead.

The final question only earns its place once the first five are answered with evidence. Scaling is what you
do *after* the dogs eat the dog food — expanding deliberately from a beachhead you actually own, using the
follow-on map you sketched in Step 14. Scale a broken model and you just lose money faster; scale a proven
one and the earlier discipline compounds.

## Step 24 — Develop a Product Plan
**What it means.** Lay out how the product evolves beyond the MVBP: the sequence of markets and capabilities
that takes you from the beachhead into the adjacent opportunities, prioritised and staged.
**For a Foundry venture.** The plan becomes a roadmap of tickets across lanes — but it stays a *plan*: you
advance to the next market only when the current one is genuinely won (retention and unit economics, not
hope). Each new market re-runs the earlier questions in miniature; it doesn't get to skip them.
**Example.** Beachhead: post-op recovery, proven. Next: chronic-pain management (same Core dataset, new
persona) → clinic-side analytics → elite-sport recovery — each entered only after the prior cohort holds.
**Trap.** Scaling on vanity. Adding markets or features while the beachhead's retention is unproven multiplies
the leak instead of the value — premature scaling is the classic way a promising venture dies.

## Where the two frameworks meet
Disciplined Entrepreneurship gets you to a **proven, profitable beachhead**. What keeps that from eroding as
competitors notice is a durable advantage — a **Power**. The Core you named in Step 10 is the seed; the next
sections (*Moats — the seven powers*, and the deep breakdown that follows) are how a Foundry venture turns
that seed into something that lasts. Build the business with DE; defend it with the 7 Powers.
