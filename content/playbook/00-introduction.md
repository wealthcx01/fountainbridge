# The Foundry Playbook

Most "how to start a company" advice is a library of tactics with no spine — a thousand tips and no
order to apply them in. Foundry ventures don't run on tips. They run on a **method**: a repeatable
way to find a real market, build the smallest thing that proves value, sell it to people who already
want it, and compound an advantage that gets harder to copy over time.

This playbook is that method. It is not a course and it is not theory. It is the operating manual for
a Bruntsfield-co-created venture — the thinking that sits underneath the studio you sign into.

We stand on two shoulders and add our own:

- **The build** follows the discipline of Bill Aulet's *Disciplined Entrepreneurship* (MIT) — a
  step-by-step arc from "who is your customer" to "how you scale," reworked for ventures that are
  co-built with agents and sold on consent-first GTM.
- **The moat** follows Hamilton Helmer's *7 Powers* — because a business that can't defend its value
  is a treadmill. We map each Power to the stage of a venture where you can actually build it.

Both are frameworks we apply and credit; the words, examples, and the Foundry-specific method are
ours. Read them in order the first time. After that, use them as a checklist — the point of a method
is that you can tell, at any moment, what you actually know and what you're still guessing.

## The four questions this playbook answers

1. **[The build arc](build-arc)** — what to do, in what order, from idea to scale.
2. **[Moats](moats)** — how to make the value you create hard to take away.
3. **[Selling](selling)** — how a Foundry venture actually gets its first and next customers.
4. **[How we run a venture](operating-model)** — the machine (studio, lanes, gates) that does the work.

> The product is the point. This playbook is the soft introduction and the reference; the studio is
> where the work happens.
